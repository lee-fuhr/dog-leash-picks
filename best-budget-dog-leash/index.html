A leash is one of the few pieces of gear you’ll use every single walk, so finding one that’s safe, comfortable, and built to last matters, even on a budget. The good news: you don’t need to spend a lot to get a leash that won’t fray, snap, or chew up your hands. When shopping on a budget, focus on material (nylon or polyester are affordable and durable), hardware (look for solid metal clips, not plastic), and length (4 to 6 feet is standard for most walks). Avoid overly cheap leashes with thin stitching or flimsy swivels, and consider your dog’s pulling strength and size before committing to a specific width or handle style.
