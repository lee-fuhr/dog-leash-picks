How we choose our picks

We don’t run lab tests or timed trials. Instead, we spend weeks reading owner reviews from multiple retail and community sources, looking for patterns in long-term durability, what holds up after six months of daily use and what falls apart. We also cross-reference professional reviews from independent trainers and gear editors who have hands-on experience with dozens of leashes. The most important failure points we track are stitching at stress points, clip integrity, and handle padding that shifts or compresses. A leash that looks great on day one but frays after a few weeks doesn’t make our list. We prioritize brands with a reputation for honoring warranties and replacing defective gear without hassle.
