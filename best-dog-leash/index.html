A good dog leash is one of the few pieces of gear you use every single walk, rain or shine, from puppyhood to senior years. Yet many leashes wear out quickly, fray at the stitching, or dig into your hands during a hard pull. The right leash balances comfort for you, control for your dog, and enough durability to survive daily use without becoming a hassle. When choosing, focus on the material (nylon vs. leather vs. biothane), the hardware (solid brass or stainless steel snaps are worth the extra cost), and the length that fits your lifestyle. A four- to six-foot leash is the standard for neighborhood walks, while hands-free or traffic handles add versatility. Avoid anything with flimsy swivels or cheap clips that can open under pressure.
